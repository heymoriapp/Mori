## Long product description
Mori is a small, private writing companion that lives in your Mac's menu bar. Instead of switching to a chat window and re-explaining context every time, you select the text you're looking at, press **⌥M**, and Mori opens a lightweight composer right there.

From one shortcut you can **draft a reply**, **rewrite** for tone, **summarize** a long thread, or **recall** something from notes you've saved. You can turn any draft into a saved memory, then pull it back later with keyword recall. There's also an **actions palette** (⌥⇧M) for one-keystroke transforms like "fix grammar" or "make it shorter," plus your own custom actions.

Mori is **Mac-first and user-triggered** — nothing happens until you press the shortcut. It doesn't keylog, capture your screen, or run in the background watching your apps. **Memories are stored locally on your Mac by default.** For the AI itself, you bring your own key (OpenAI-compatible or Anthropic), and **AI processing depends on the provider and settings you choose** — Mori sends only the context you decide to generate with.

It's early: v0.1.0 is a private beta. Selected-text capture and insert behavior can vary by app, recall is keyword-based, and drafting is a remote API call (not on-device). But the core loop — *context → ⌥M → draft → copy/insert* — is here, and we'd love your feedback.

## Founder note
I built Mori because the tools I loved for writing all lived in a *different* window than the one I was writing in. Every reply meant leaving the conversation to go explain the conversation.

So Mori is deliberately small and local-leaning: it lives in the menu bar, it only acts when you press ⌥M, and your saved memory stays on your Mac by default. I've tried hard not to overclaim — it's not magic background memory, and the AI still runs through a provider you choose and pay for. What it *is* is a fast ⌥M away from a good first draft with the right context.

This is the first private beta (v0.1.0). It has rough edges, and that's the point — I want to fix the ones that matter to real Mac writers. If that's you, I'd genuinely value your notes.

— The Mori team
